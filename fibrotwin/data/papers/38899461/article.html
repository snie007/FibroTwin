Abstract

Background:

Cardiac fibroblast activation contributes to adverse remodeling, fibrosis, and dysfunction in the pressure-overloaded heart. Although early fibroblast TGF-β/Smad3 activation protects the pressure overloaded heart by preserving the matrix, sustained TGF-β activation is deleterious, accentuating fibrosis and dysfunction. Thus, endogenous mechanisms that negatively regulate the TGF-β response in fibroblasts may be required to protect from progressive fibrosis and adverse remodeling. We hypothesized that Smad7, an inhibitory Smad that restrains TGF-β signaling, may be induced in the pressure-overloaded myocardium and may regulate fibrosis, remodeling and dysfunction.

Methods:

The effects of myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss were studied in a mouse model of transverse aortic constriction (TAC), using echocardiography, histological and molecular analysis. Proteomic studies in Smad7 knockout and overexpressing cells were used to identify fibroblast-derived mediators modulated by Smad7. In vitro experiments using cultured cardiac fibroblasts, fibroblasts populating collagen lattices and isolated macrophages were used to dissect the molecular signals responsible for the effects of Smad7.

Results:

Following pressure overload, Smad7 was upregulated in cardiac myofibroblasts. TGF-β and angiotensin II stimulated fibroblast Smad7 upregulation via Smad3, whereas GDF15 induced Smad7 through GFRAL. Myofibroblast-specific Smad7 knockout mice (MFS7KO) had increased mortality, accentuated systolic dysfunction and dilative remodeling, and accelerated diastolic dysfunction in response to TAC. Increased dysfunction in MFS7KO hearts was associated with accentuated fibrosis, and increased matrix metalloproteinase (MMP)-2 activity and collagen denaturation. Secretomic analysis showed that Smad7 loss accentuates secretion of structural collagens and matricellular proteins, and markedly increases MMP-2 secretion. In contrast, Smad7 overexpression reduced MMP2 levels. In fibroblasts populating collagen lattices, the effects of Smad7 on fibroblast-induced collagen denaturation and pad contraction were partly mediated via MMP2 downregulation. Surprisingly, MFS7KO mice also exhibited significant macrophage expansion caused by paracrine actions of Smad7 null fibroblasts that stimulate macrophage proliferation and fibrogenic activation. Macrophage activation involved the combined effects of the fibroblast-derived matricellular proteins CD5L, SPARC, CTGF, ECM1 and TGFBI.

Conclusions:

The anti-fibrotic effects of Smad7 in the pressure-overloaded heart protect from dysfunction and involve not only reduction in collagen deposition, but also suppression of MMP2-mediated matrix denaturation and paracrine effects that suppress macrophage activation through inhibition of matricellular proteins.

INTRODUCTION:

Expansion and activation of interstitial fibroblasts is critically involved in the cardiac response to pressure overload1, 2,3. When subjected to mechanical stress, resident fibroblast lineages proliferate and undergo myofibroblast conversion4, 5. These activated interstitial cells initially serve to protect the pressure-overloaded myocardium by preserving the integrity of the extracellular matrix (ECM), preventing further release of matrix fragments that exacerbate inflammation and accentuate cardiomyocyte injury6. However, unrestrained chronic activation of myofibroblasts has detrimental effects by promoting a matrix-synthetic program with progressive deposition of interstitial and perivascular collagen, causing ventricular dysfunction and exacerbating heart failure7–9. There is a need to understand the negative regulatory mechanisms that inhibit fibroblast activity, preventing excessive, prolonged or inappropriate activation and protecting from fibrosis progression and functional decompensation.

The members of the transforming growth factor β (TGF-β) family are central mediators of fibroblast activation10, 11. TGF-βs signal by sequentially transphosphorylating type II and type I receptors, activating downstream signaling cascades that involve intracellular receptor-activated Smads (R-Smads), or Smad-independent pathways12, 13. The inhibitory Smad, Smad7, is a critical endogenous negative regulator of TGF-β signaling. Smad7 lacks the C-terminal SSXS motif and cannot be phosphorylated by TGF-β type I receptors but is able to inhibit TGF-β family signaling through interfering with the interaction between TGF-β receptors, downstream receptor-Smads 2/3 and common-Smad4 proteins14, 15. Although early studies have attempted to study the role of Smad7 in models of hypertensive cardiomyopathy16, 17, their findings are limited by the absence of cell-specific approaches and by the use of a hypomorphic global Smad7 mutant line (Smad7Δex1) that exhibits preserved Smad7 functions due to the presence of an intact MH2 domain that is involved in Smad7 interactions with TGF-β signaling18. Our recent study used cell-specific approaches to demonstrate a central role of myofibroblast-specific Smad7 in protecting from post-infarction remodeling and suggested that Smad7 acts not only by targeting the TGF-β cascade, but also through TGF-independent actions on Erbb219. However, the role of Smad7 in fibrosis and dysfunction in the pressure-overloaded heart remains unknown.

In the current study, we hypothesized that fibroblast-specific Smad7 induction may play a central role in regulating fibrosis, remodeling and dysfunction in the pressure-overloaded heart. Using myofibroblast-specific loss of function approaches in a model of pressure overload induced through transverse aortic constriction (TAC), we found that Smad7 is markedly upregulated in activated myofibroblasts and protects the heart from fibrosis and development of systolic and diastolic dysfunction. Surprisingly, the beneficial actions of fibroblast Smad7 were not only due to the expected inhibition of ECM deposition but also involved suppression of matrix metalloproteinase-2 (MMP2)-mediated matrix denaturation and inhibition of paracrine fibrogenic effects of fibroblasts on cardiac macrophages. Our findings reveal a novel fibroblast-macrophage axis involved in regulation of the fibrotic response following pressure overload and highlight the importance of timely induction of endogenous protective signals that prevent fibroblast overactivity, excessive ECM degradation and fibrogenic macrophage activation.

METHODS

Data availability statement:

The data supporting the findings of the study are available from the corresponding author upon reasonable request. The mass spectrometry proteomic data have been deposited to the ProteomeXchange Consortium via the PRIDE partner repository with the dataset identifiers PXD043577 and PXD049057.

Detailed description of the methodology is provided in the online supplement

Animal studies were approved by the Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee at Albert Einstein College of Medicine and conform to the Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals (National Academies Press, 2011). Both male and female fibroblast reporter PDGFRaEGFP mice, animals with myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss19 and Smad7 fl/fl controls were used for in vivo experiments. To induce left ventricular pressure overload in mice, we used a model of TAC. The surgical protocols were performed by an investigator blinded to the genotype of the animals. Cardiac function and chamber dimensions were assessed echocardiographically with the Vevo 2100 system (VisualSonics). The carotid flow ratio was used to confirm comparable pressure gradients between groups. Histological, immunohistochemical and immunofluorescence techniques were used to study the cellular composition, matrix content, collagen denaturation, cardiomyocyte size and apoptosis in the pressure-overloaded heart. The list of antibodies used in the study is provided in Table S1.

Fibroblasts, isolated from 12-week-old mouse hearts, and bone marrow macrophages (BMMs) were used for stimulation experiments. For Smad7 deletion, mouse cardiac fibroblasts isolated from Smad7 fl/fl mice were transfected with adenovirus expressing Cre, to knock out the loxP-flanked Smad7 gene. For Smad7 overexpression experiments, mouse cardiac fibroblasts were transfected with a plasmid containing a Myc-DDK–tagged mouse Smad7 cDNA clone (Origene, #MG226590). To study the role of overactive MMP2 in mediating the effects of Smad7 loss in fibroblasts, we cultured cardiac fibroblasts (Control and S7KO) in collagen pads.

Protein from whole sham and TAC hearts was used for Western blotting. Proteomic analysis was performed on the conditioned media (supernatant) from control (WT) and Smad7KO fibroblasts, or from WT and S7OE fibroblasts. Peptides of each sample were analyzed through liquid chromatographic separation with a two-column system in tandem with mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS). MMP2 activity in the pressure overloaded heart was assessed by gelatin zymography, and by a MMP-2 fluorometric substrate activity assay. RNA analysis was performed using qPCR with custom-made primers, or commercially available PCR arrays. The primers used are listed in Table S2.

Statistics:

Normal distribution was tested using the Shapiro–Wilk normality test. For comparisons of two groups unpaired two-tailed Student’s t test using (when appropriate) Welch’s correction for unequal variances was performed. The Mann–Whitney test was used for comparisons between two groups that did not show Gaussian distribution. For comparisons of multiple groups, one-way ANOVA, 2-way ANOVA, or 2-way repeated measures ANOVA was performed followed by Sidak multiple comparison test. The Kruskal–Wallis test, followed by Dunn’s multiple comparison posttest was used when one or more groups did not show Gaussian distribution. For in vitro experiments where each experimental dataset is an average of thousands to millions of cells, we assumed the data were normally distributed based on the central limit theorem. Survival analysis was performed using the Kaplan–Meier method. Mortality was compared using the log rank test. Associations between echocardiographic and histologic end points were studied with Pearson’s correlation analysis. Data are expressed as means ± SEM. Statistical significance was set at P < 0.05. Statistical analysis was performed with GraphPad Prism 10.

RESULTS:

1. Smad7 is upregulated in fibroblasts and myofibroblasts in the pressure-overloaded heart.

Myocardial Smad7 protein was upregulated after 3 days of TAC, remained elevated after 7 days and increased further after 28 days of TAC (Figure 1A). Smad7 immunoreactivity was localized in cardiomyocytes, vascular and interstitial cells (Figure S1). To examine whether Smad7-expressing interstitial cells are fibroblasts, we used PDGFRαEGFP fibroblast reporter mice. Numerous PDGFRα+/Smad7+ cardiac fibroblasts were identified 7–56 days after TAC (Figure 1B). Myofibroblasts, identified as interstitial α-SMA+ cells located outside the vascular media exhibited high Smad7 immunoreactivity (Figure 1C).

2. TGF-βs, BMP4, GDF15 and angiotensin II, induce Smad7 upregulation in cardiac fibroblasts.

Next, we identified mediators inducing fibroblast Smad7 upregulation in isolated cardiac fibroblasts. TGF-β1 and GDF15 induced Smad7 upregulation at both 4h and 24h timepoints (Figure 1D–E). TGF-β2 stimulated early, but not late, Smad7 upregulation (Figure 1D), whereas TGF-β3, BMP4 and Angiotensin II induced late Smad7 expression (Figure 1E).

3. TGF-β- and angiotensin II-mediated fibroblast Smad7 induction is dependent on Smad3.

Pharmacologic inhibition experiments using the dual TGF-β type I/II receptor inhibitor LY2109761 and the Smad3 inhibitor SIS3 demonstrated that both early and late Smad7 upregulation in response to TGF-β1 was dependent on TβR and Smad3 signaling. On the other hand, late angiotensin II-induced Smad7 upregulation after 24h of stimulation was TβR-independent but involved Smad3 activation (Figure S2)

4. GDF15-induced Smad7 upregulation is mediated through GFRAL

GDF15 signals either through TβRs, or by activating the GDNF Family receptor α like (GFRAL). Pharmacologic inhibition and antibody neutralization experiments showed that both early and late GDF15-induced fibroblast Smad7 upregulation was independent of TβRs but required GFRAL. Late, but not early GDF15-imediated Smad7 induction also involved Smad3 (Figure S2).

5. Myofibroblast-specific Smad7 protects the pressure-overloaded heart from death, systolic dysfunction, and dilation, and delays the onset of diastolic dysfunction.

To study the role of myofibroblast Smad7 in the pressure-overloaded heart, we generated mice with myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss (MFS7KO) using the periostin Cre driver. Dual immunofluorescence demonstrated Smad7 loss, specifically in periostin+ myofibroblasts in MSF7KO pressure-overloaded hearts after TAC (Figure S3). Dual immunofluorescence for α-SMA/Smad7 confirmed the myofibroblast-specific loss of Smad7 in MFS7KO mice (Figure S4). Baseline ventricular function was not affected by myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss. After TAC, Smad7 fl/fl and MFS7KO mice had comparable right to left carotid ratios, indicating similar pressure gradients (Figure S5A). When compared with Smad7 fl/fl mice, MFS7KO mice had significantly increased mortality after pressure overload (p=0.021, Figure 2A). MFS7KO mice had worse systolic dysfunction, suggested by a reduction in left-ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) and a higher ΔLVEF after 56 days of TAC (Figure 2B–C). Moreover, MSF7KO had accentuated chamber dilation, evidenced by a significant increase in left ventricular end-diastolic volume (LVEDV) at 28 days and by increased ΔLVEDV at all timepoints, when compared to Smad7 fl/fl mice (Figure 2D–E). The increase in left ventricular (LV) mass was comparable between Smad7 fl/fl and MFS7KO mice (Figure S5C–D). Myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss was also associated with an increased E/E′ ratio after 7 days of TAC. This difference was transient and was not observed at later timepoints (28-and 56 days after TAC), suggesting that myofibroblast Smad7 delays the development of diastolic dysfunction (Figure 2F).

6. Sex-specific protective effects of myofibroblast Smad7.

Although myofibroblast-specific loss of Smad7 was associated with worse dysfunction and increased dilation in response to pressure overload in both male (Figure 2H–K) and female mice (Figure 2N–Q), only male MFS7KO animals had increased mortality (Figure 2G, M). Moreover, male but not female MFS7KO mice had increased E/E’ (suggesting worse diastolic dysfunction) after 7 days of TAC (Figure 2L, R). The findings suggest that, although myofibroblast Smad7 protects from systolic dysfunction and dilative remodeling in both male and female mice, its pro-survival effects and the effects on diastolic function are restricted to male animals.

7. Myofibroblast-specific Smad7 protects the pressure-overloaded myocardium from excessive collagen deposition and denaturation.

We examined whether the effects of myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss on cardiac function may reflect increased deposition of collagens, or perturbations in collagen remodeling. 56 days after TAC, MFS7KO mice had significantly higher collagen content than S7 fl/fl animals (Figure 3A–B). The pro-fibrotic effects of myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss were observed in both male and female MFS7KO mice (Figure S6A–B). To examine whether myofibroblast Smad7 loss affects collagen denaturation, we performed staining with CHP (collagen hybridizing peptide), which specifically binds to denatured unfolded collagen20. Both male and female MFS7KO mice had increased collagen denaturation after 56 days of TAC (Figure 3C–D, Figure S6C–D). Thus, myofibroblast Smad7 restrains both collagen deposition and denaturation. Accentuated fibrosis in pressure-overloaded MFS7KO mice was predominantly interstitial (Figure 3). No significant effects of myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss on perivascular fibrosis were found (Figure S7). The severity of systolic dysfunction, diastolic dysfunction and dilative remodeling in MFS7KO animals (56 days after TAC) was associated with the extent of collagen deposition (Figure S8–9). Collagen content positively correlated with LVEDV, LVESV and LV mass, suggesting a relation between fibrosis and adverse remodeling (both dilative and hypertrophic) (Figure S8C–E). A negative correlation was noted between collagen content and LVEF, consistent with an association between the extent of fibrosis and systolic dysfunction (Figure S8B). A positive correlation between collagen content and E/E’ suggested an association between fibrosis and diastolic dysfunction (Figure S8F). Next, we examined the effects of fibroblast-specific Smad7 loss on matrix gene expression. MFS7KO mice exhibited increased expression of Col1a1, Col3a1 and Mmp2, 56 days after TAC (Figure 3E–H) and higher Timp1 levels at both 7d and 56d timepoints.

8. Smad7 does not affect mature myofibroblast density after TAC but restrains periostin deposition.

Myofibroblast density significantly increased in the pressure-overloaded heart in both S7 fl/fl and MFS7KO groups. After 56 days of TAC, MFS7KO had a trend toward increased myofibroblast density that did not reach statistical significance. Sex-specific breakdown showed a modest (~20%), but significant increase in myofibroblast density in male, but not in female, MFS7KO mice after 56 days of pressure overload (Figure S10). Myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss increased expression of periostin, an earlier marker of myofibroblast conversion, in both male and female TAC animals (Figure S11A–B).

9. Myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss does not affect cardiomyocyte apoptosis and hypertrophy.

We found no significant differences in the density of TUNEL+ apoptotic cardiomyocytes between MFS7KO and S7 fl/fl mice after TAC (Figure S12). Moreover, myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss had no significant effects on cardiomyocyte size and did not affect expression of the heart failure-associated cardiomyocyte genes Anp, Bnp and Myh7, all markedly upregulated in both Smad7 fl/fl and MFS7KO pressure-overloaded hearts (Figure S13).

10. Myofibroblast-specific Smad7 restrains macrophage infiltration and proliferation in the pressure-overloaded myocardium.

MFS7KO mice had a transient 2.5-fold increase in macrophage density in comparison to S7 fl/fl mice, after 7 days of TAC (Figure 4A–B). The effects of myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss on macrophage infiltration were noted in both male and female mice (Figure 4C–D). Levels of the macrophage-specific gene Adgre1 (encoding F4/80) and the macrophage chemoattractant chemokine Ccl2 were significantly higher in MFS7KO mice after 7 days of TAC (Figure 4E–F). To examine whether macrophage expansion in pressure-overloaded MFS7KO hearts was due to effects on proliferation and/or apoptosis of these cells, we performed dual immunofluorescence combining the macrophage marker Mac2 with the proliferation marker Ki67, or with TUNEL. MFS7KO mice had a significant increase in proliferating macrophages after TAC, when compared to S7fl/fl animals (Figure S14A–E). In contrast, no significant differences in macrophage apoptosis were noted (Figure S14F–J). We reasoned that because myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss did not affect cardiomyocyte loss (Figure S12), macrophage expansion in pressure-overloaded MFS7KO mice is not driven by cardiomyocyte death but may be due to paracrine actions of fibroblasts on macrophages.

11. Smad7 restrains fibroblast secretion of structural and matricellular proteins.

To identify the fibroblast-secreted mediators responsible for the anti-fibrotic effects of Smad7, we analyzed and compared the secretome of Smad7KO and WT fibroblasts, in the presence and absence of TGF-β (24 h stimulation). When ranked by protein fold increase in S7KO supernatant compared to WT (in presence of TGF-β stimulation), we found 8 proteins significantly upregulated by Smad7 loss (Figure 5A). In unstimulated cells, 13 proteins were significantly increased in the supernatant of Smad7KO compared to WT (p< 0.05) (Figure 5B). The gelatinase MMP2, the structural matrix proteins COL1A1 and COL3A1 and the matricellular glycoproteins TGF-β-induced (TGFBI), ECM protein 1 (ECM1) and CD5 antigen-like (CD5L) were significantly increased in the absence of Smad7, in both TGF-β-stimulated and control cells (Figure 5A–B). Fibronectin (FN1) and the precursor of sphingolipid activators (PSAP) were significantly upregulated by Smad7 loss only in TGF-β-stimulated cells. On the other hand, several ECM proteins and growth factors were significantly increased in unstimulated Smad7 KO cells, when compared with unstimulated controls, including EFEMP1 (associated EGF-containing fibulin-like extracellular matrix protein 1), PCOLCE (Procollagen C-endopeptidase enhancer), FBLN2 (Fibulin-2), SPARC (Secreted protein acidic and rich in cysteine), CCN2/CTGF (connective tissue growth factor), IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor); and COL5A2.

Next, we examined whether the effects of Smad7 loss on the increased level of these proteins are due to increased transcription. Analysis of our published RNA-seq data19 (GSE185767) comparing the transcriptome of Smad7 KO fibroblasts versus control cardiac fibroblasts, showed that Smad7 loss was associated with increased Tgfbi, Cd5l and Psap. In contrast, Mmp2, Ctgf, Col5a2, Sparc, Pcolce and Efemp1 were downregulated in S7KO cells treated with TGFβ1, whereas Fn1, Col3a1 and Fbln2 levels were not affected by Smad7 loss (Figure S15). Thus, the effects of Smad7 loss on the secretomic profile of fibroblasts are only in part explained by modulation of transcription.

12. Effects of Smad7 overexpression on the fibroblast secretome.

Smad7 levels were markedly increased in Smad7 OE cells (Figure S16). In the presence of TGF-β1, 20 proteins were significantly upregulated and 55 proteins were significantly downregulated by Smad7 overexpression (Table S3). In unstimulated cells, 46 proteins were upregulated and 106 proteins were downregulated upon Smad7 overexpression (Table S4). Out of the proteins found to be upregulated in Smad7 KO cells, MMP2 and COL3A1 were significantly downregulated upon Smad7 overexpression (Figure 5C). COL1A1 levels were reduced in Smad7 overexpressing cells; however, the reduction did not reach statistical significance (p=0.06). In contrast, TGFBI, CD5L, FN1, ECM1 and PSAP (all significantly upregulated in Smad7 KO cells) were not modulated by Smad7 overexpression. Smad7 overexpression reduced levels of several other ECM proteins and fibrogenic mediators (not modulated by Smad7 loss), including DPT/dermatopontin, CCN2/CTGF, TGF-β1 and SPP1/osteopontin (Figure S16).

13. The antifibrotic effects of fibroblast Smad7 involve suppression of MMP-mediated matrix degradation

The top-ranked secreted mediator upregulated upon Smad7 loss is MMP2, a matrix-degrading protease. To examine the role of Smad7-regulated MMP2 in fibroblast-mediated matrix remodeling, we compared the effects of MMP2 inhibition on collagen deposition and denaturation in TGF-β-treated Smad7KO and WT fibroblasts cultured in collagen lattices. (Figure 6). Consistent with the in vivo findings, Smad7 loss increased both collagen deposition and denaturation in TGF-treated fibroblast-populated lattices. Moreover, Smad7 loss accentuated fibroblast-induced pad contraction (in the presence or absence of TGF-β1) (Figure 6K–M), MMP2 inhibition attenuated the increase in collagen denaturation in pads populated with Smad7 KO cells (Figure 6E) but had no significant effects on collagen deposition (Figure 6J). Moreover, MMP2 inhibition abrogated the effects of Smad7 loss on pad contraction in TGF-β1-treated lattices (Figure 6K–M). Thus, the effects of Smad7 on fibroblast-mediated collagen denaturation and on pad contraction are mediated, at least in part, via downregulation of MMP2 secretion. In contrast, Smad7 overexpression attenuated collagen deposition and denaturation (Figure 7A–B), and significantly reduced lattice contraction (Figure 7C).

14. In vivo, myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss increases MMP2 activity.

Using zymography, we found that pro-MMP2 levels are increased in TAC hearts after 7–56 days of TAC but are not affected by fibroblast-specific Smad7 loss. Active MMP2 levels were significantly increased 56 days after TAC and were accentuated in MFS7KO. A fluorometric MMP2 substrate assay confirmed these observations, showing significantly higher active MMP2 levels in MFS7KO mice at the 56-day timepoint. In contrast, pro-MMP9 and active MMP9 levels did not significantly increase after TAC and were not affected by myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss (Figure S17).

15. The antifibrotic effects of fibroblast Smad7 may involve suppression of CCN2 autocrine effects.

In addition to their effects on the matrix, secreted fibroblast-derived mediators may also exert fibrogenic effects through autocrine actions. To identify autocrine mechanisms involved in fibroblast phenotypic modulation, we performed Ingenuity pathway analysis of the secretomic data. Upstream regulator analysis identified mediators that may be involved in the differential secretomic profile of S7KO vs WT fibroblasts. The top 10 upstream regulators (ranked by p value) are listed in Table S5. The list includes TGFβ1 and EGFR, consistent with our previously reported effects of Smad7 in restraining TGFβ and RTK signaling19. When ranked by activation z-score (z-score>2.0), several upstream regulators were predicted to be activated in Smad7 KO fibroblasts (Table S6), including RICTOR, LARP1, KLF3, IL10RA, SKIV2L, MNT, GRIN1, RORC, EGLN, CCN2, ITGB3, CLPP, TEAD3, ITGA11 and TEAD2. To identify autocrine upstream activators, we examined whether any of these factors were secreted mediators, upregulated upon Smad7 loss. The fibrogenic matricellular protein CCN2 (cellular communication network factor 2)/CTGF is markedly upregulated in the secretome of S7KO fibroblasts (Figure 5) and may serve as a key regulator of their proteomic profile.

16. Paracrine actions of Smad7 KO fibroblasts on macrophages stimulate proliferation and induce a fibrogenic phenotype.

To examine whether Smad7KO fibroblasts exert paracrine effects on macrophages, we stimulated isolated BMMs with the supernatant from cultured WT and Smad7KO fibroblasts, and we examined effects on proliferation and on expression of inflammatory and fibrosis-associated genes.

Under conditions favoring their proliferation (15% Serum + 120ng/mL CSF-1), macrophages treated with the supernatant of Smad7KO fibroblast had significantly increased proliferative activity when compared to untreated macrophages (control) and showed a trend towards increased proliferation (p=0.067) in comparison to macrophages treated with control fibroblast supernatant (Figure 8). The supernatant of S7KO fibroblasts stimulated a fibrogenic macrophage phenotype, inducing synthesis of Tgfb1, and upregulating expression of Cd206 and Pparg, both markers of fibrogenic M2 macrophages (Figure 8B–D). Moreover, S7KO fibroblast supernatant stimulated Vegfa (a M2 macrophage product) and downmodulated synthesis of the M1-associated neutrophil chemoattractant Cxcl1 (Figure 8E–F). Smad7 loss had no effect on other pro-inflammatory genes, such as Ccl2, Il1b, Tnfa, Csfr1, Nos2, Ccl5, Ifng, Il12, Icam1 and Vcam1 (Figure S18).

To dissect the molecular mechanisms for the macrophage-activating effects of Smad7 null fibroblasts, we identified candidate mediators based on their increased secretion in the absence of Smad7 (Figure 5) and their reported effects on macrophage phenotype. Of the proteins found to be secreted by Smad7KO fibroblasts, CD5L, CTGF, SPARC and TGFBI, are known modulators of macrophage phenotype and activity (Table S7). Accordingly, we examined whether stimulation with one of these mediators recapitulates the fibrogenic actions of the supernatant of S7KO fibroblasts on BMMs (Figure 8I–M and Figure S19). Of the 5 mediators tested, only CCN2/CTGF and TGFBI induced expression of Tgfb1 by macrophages, whereas CCN2/CTGF and SPARC stimulated expression of the M2 marker Cd206. Only SPARC stimulated expression of Pparg. Moreover, CCN2/CTGF, CD5L and ECM1 increased Vegfa synthesis, whereas CCN2/CTGF and SPARC recapitulated the suppressive effects of the S7KO fibroblast supernatant on Cxcl1 expression. These results suggest that the paracrine activating effects of myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss on macrophages are not due to induction of a single mediator but may rather involve the combined actions of CCN2/CTGF, SPARC, TGFBI, CD5L and ECM1.

DISCUSSION

We demonstrate for the first time that myofibroblast Smad7 induction protects the pressure-overloaded heart, attenuating systolic dysfunction, delaying diastolic dysfunction, and inhibiting fibrotic remodeling. Analysis of the fibroblast secretomic profile and in vitro experiments demonstrated that the beneficial actions of myofibroblast Smad7 are not mediated only through the (predictable) suppression of TGF-β- and Erbb2-mediated ECM deposition but may also involve 2 additional novel mechanisms. First, fibroblast Smad7 restrains the secretion of matrix-degrading proteases, such as MMP2, by activated fibroblasts, thus attenuating collagen denaturation. Second, fibroblast Smad7 inhibits paracrine activation of macrophages by attenuating secretion of several matricellular macrophage-stimulating proteins (such as SPARC, CCN2 and TGFBI). Our findings highlight the importance of timely induction of protective signals in fibroblasts populating the pressure-overloaded heart, to prevent excessive ECM degradation and attenuate inflammatory cell activation.

Endogenous inhibitory signals restrain TGF-β-mediated fibrosis.

Although early effects of TGF-βs are initially protective6,21,22 prolonged activation of TGF-β cascades following myocardial injury worsens systolic and diastolic dysfunction23,24,25. TGF-β effects on fibroblasts need to be tightly regulated, to prevent myofibroblast overactivation and excessive ECM deposition that could accentuate fibrosis, increasing ventricular stiffness. The inhibitory Smad, Smad7 is a critical negative feedback mechanism that restrains TGF-mediated signaling26. Our experiments showed that in cardiac fibroblasts, the TGF-β superfamily members TGF-β1 and GDF15 induce rapid and prolonged upregulation of SMAD7. Although the effects of TGF-β1 are dependent on TβRs and Smad3, GDF15 actions involve GFRAL signaling (Figure 1, Figure S2). We also explored for the first time the fibroblast-specific effects of endogenous Smad7 in pressure overloaded-hearts. We found that myofibroblast Smad7 protects the heart from fibrosis and from the development of systolic and diastolic dysfunction. Although the inhibitory effects of Smad7 on collagen deposition are predictable, our experiments identified two additional novel mechanisms that may mediate the protective actions of fibroblast Smad7.

Smad7 inhibits fibroblast matrix-degrading activity suppressing MMP2 release.

The protein exhibiting the highest level of overexpression upon Smad7 loss was MMP2, a protease that cleaves a broad spectrum of ECM components, including collagens, laminin, and fibronectin27. MMP2 neutralization in isolated fibroblasts populating collagen lattices (Figure 6) suggested that increased MMP2 expression may be responsible, at least in part, for the accentuated collagen denaturation in pressure-overloaded myofibroblast-specific Smad7 KO hearts. In vivo studies have demonstrated that MMP2 plays a central role in the pathogenesis of adverse remodeling and dysfunction following pressure overload28, 29. Several mechanisms may be responsible for the deleterious effects of MMP2. First, MMP2-driven generation of cleaved ECM fragments may promote pro-inflammatory signaling and deprive cardiomyocytes from important survival signals. Second, ECM degradation may perturb matrix-dependent mechanical support leading to chamber dilation. Third, MMP2 has a broad range of ECM-independent targets30. For example, cleavage of cardiomyocyte membrane proteins (such as junctophilin-2)31 by fibroblast secreted MMP2 may perturb calcium handling and impair contraction, contributing to the detrimental myocardial actions of fibroblast Smad7 loss.

Fibroblast-derived matricellular proteins promote a fibrogenic macrophage phenotype.

Myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss was also associated with a 2.5-fold increase in macrophage density, driven by acquisition of a proliferative macrophage phenotype. To examine whether fibroblasts modulate macrophage phenotype through paracrine actions, we stimulated isolated BMMs with the supernatant of cultured Smad7 KO vs WT fibroblasts. The supernatant of Smad7 KO fibroblasts increased expression of fibrogenic M2-like macrophage markers, such as Tgfb1, Vegfa32, 33, Cd206 and Pparg34, 35, and also induced a trend towards increased proliferation. Thus, Smad7 loss in fibroblasts may activate a fibrogenic macrophage-driven feed-forward loop that accentuates fibroblast activation and promotes fibrosis.

Macrophages and fibroblasts can secrete cytokines and growth factors to support their mutual activation36–39. Which paracrine signals are involved in macrophage activation triggered by fibroblast Smad7 loss? Our secretomic analysis, combined with in vitro experiments identified 5 fibroblast-derived matricellular proteins that may be responsible for the paracrine actions of the fibroblasts: CCN2, SPARC, TGFBI, CD5L and ECM1. None of these mediators recapitulated all effects of the Smad7 KO fibroblast supernatant; thus, the observed macrophage phenotype likely reflects the combined actions of the mediators. However, the actions of CCN2 mediated most of the effects of Smad7 KO fibroblast supernatant on macrophages, increasing expression of Tgfb1, Cd206, Vegfa and Pparg. Several studies have suggested an important role for CCN2 in fibrotic tissue remodeling40, 41, and in fibrosis in the pressure overloaded myocardium42; however, other investigations have produced negative results43. Moreover, the in vivo significance of the effects of CCN2 on macrophage activation has not been documented.

Does the functional diversity of cardiac fibroblasts reflect their phenotypic heterogeneity?

Our findings highlight the diverse functions of fibroblasts in cardiac pathologic conditions44,45. In addition to their prominent role in ECM secretion and metabolism, fibroblasts can modulate phenotype and function of other cell types, such as cardiomyocytes, macrophages and vascular cells, thus regulating cardiomyocyte survival, inflammation and angiogenesis. Single cell studies have revealed the transcriptomic heterogeneity of cardiac fibroblast populations in injured hearts46,47,48,49; however, the relations between the various transcriptional profiles and specific functions is unclear. Whether modulation of macrophage phenotype involves specific fibroblast subpopulations, which are critically regulated by Smad7 is unknown.

Sex-specific effects of Smad7 loss

Our findings suggest that myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss is associated with a striking sex-specific effect on mortality, despite comparable fibrotic responses between sexes (Figure 3, Figure S6). Considering that the severity of systolic dysfunction in the TAC model is not sufficient to induce a low output heart failure death, the finding may reflect a higher susceptibility of male mice to arrhythmia or conduction abnormalities in response to pressure overload. The association between myocardial fibrosis and arrhythmogenesis is well-established; however, whether female sex can protect from the pathogenesis of fibrosis-associated dysrhythmias is not known.

Conclusions

Induction of endogenous signals that restrain fibrogenic TGF-β and Erbb2 cascades (such as Smad7) protects the failing heart from the catastrophic effects of overactive fibroblasts. In addition to its effects as an inhibitor of ECM deposition, Smad7 also attenuates collagen denaturation by reducing MMP2 secretion, and suppresses release of matricellular proteins with macrophage-activating functions. Thus, fibroblast Smad7 has a broad range of anti-fibrotic actions and could be a promising therapeutic target in heart failure patients with prominent fibrosis.

NOVELTY AND SIGNIFICANCE:

What is known?

  • Persistent activation of fibroblasts and myofibroblasts mediates adverse remodeling and dysfunction in failing hearts.

  • Transforming Growth Factor (TGF)-β signaling cascades are critically involved in fibroblast activation.

What new information does the article contribute?

  • Endogenous induction of Smad7 in cardiac myofibroblasts attenuates adverse remodeling in the pressure-overloaded heart, restraining fibrosis.

  • The protective effects of fibroblast Smad7 on the extracellular matrix network involve suppression of matrix degradation through inhibition of Matrix Metalloproteinase 2 (MMP2).

  • Protective anti-fibrotic actions of fibroblast Smad7 also involve paracrine effects on macrophage phenotype, mediated through matricellular proteins.

Understanding the endogenous molecular signals that restrain fibrosis of the remodeling pressure-overloaded heart is critical to develop strategies that protect from heart failure progression. We investigated the role of the inhibitory Smad (I-Smad), Smad7, an intracellular inhibitor of TGF-β and ERBB2 signaling, in regulation of fibrosis and adverse remodeling upon left ventricular pressure overload. We show for the first time that Smad7 is upregulated in cardiac myofibroblasts in the pressure-overloaded heart and serves as a protective signal that restrains fibrosis and attenuates adverse remodeling and dysfunction. The anti-fibrotic effects of myofibroblast Smad7 involve actions on the extracellular matrix and paracrine effects on macrophages. Fibroblast Smad7 markedly attenuates matrix degradation by inhibiting matrix metalloproteinase 2 expression and secretion. Moreover, myofibroblast Smad7 suppresses fibroblast-derived synthesis of several matricellular macromolecules involved in fibrogenic activation of macrophages. Thus, in the pressure-overloaded heart fibroblasts acquire protective anti-fibrotic phenotypes that restrain matrix remodeling and prevent inappropriate fibrogenic activation of cardiac macrophages. Strategies accentuating endogenous anti-fibrotic signals may hold therapeutic promise in chronic heart failure. Moreover, defective activation of inhibitory anti-fibrotic pathways (such as Smad7) may be implicated in heart failure progression in heart failure patient subpopulations characterized by prominent fibrosis.

Data Availability Statement

The data supporting the findings of the study are available from the corresponding author upon reasonable request. The mass spectrometry proteomic data have been deposited to the ProteomeXchange Consortium via the PRIDE partner repository with the dataset identifiers PXD043577 and PXD049057.

Detailed description of the methodology is provided in the online supplement

Animal studies were approved by the Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee at Albert Einstein College of Medicine and conform to the Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals (National Academies Press, 2011). Both male and female fibroblast reporter PDGFRaEGFP mice, animals with myofibroblast-specific Smad7 loss19 and Smad7 fl/fl controls were used for in vivo experiments. To induce left ventricular pressure overload in mice, we used a model of TAC. The surgical protocols were performed by an investigator blinded to the genotype of the animals. Cardiac function and chamber dimensions were assessed echocardiographically with the Vevo 2100 system (VisualSonics). The carotid flow ratio was used to confirm comparable pressure gradients between groups. Histological, immunohistochemical and immunofluorescence techniques were used to study the cellular composition, matrix content, collagen denaturation, cardiomyocyte size and apoptosis in the pressure-overloaded heart. The list of antibodies used in the study is provided in Table S1.

Fibroblasts, isolated from 12-week-old mouse hearts, and bone marrow macrophages (BMMs) were used for stimulation experiments. For Smad7 deletion, mouse cardiac fibroblasts isolated from Smad7 fl/fl mice were transfected with adenovirus expressing Cre, to knock out the loxP-flanked Smad7 gene. For Smad7 overexpression experiments, mouse cardiac fibroblasts were transfected with a plasmid containing a Myc-DDK–tagged mouse Smad7 cDNA clone (Origene, #MG226590). To study the role of overactive MMP2 in mediating the effects of Smad7 loss in fibroblasts, we cultured cardiac fibroblasts (Control and S7KO) in collagen pads.

Protein from whole sham and TAC hearts was used for Western blotting. Proteomic analysis was performed on the conditioned media (supernatant) from control (WT) and Smad7KO fibroblasts, or from WT and S7OE fibroblasts. Peptides of each sample were analyzed through liquid chromatographic separation with a two-column system in tandem with mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS). MMP2 activity in the pressure overloaded heart was assessed by gelatin zymography, and by a MMP-2 fluorometric substrate activity assay. RNA analysis was performed using qPCR with custom-made primers, or commercially available PCR arrays. The primers used are listed in Table S2.
